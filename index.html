How Turoi's methodology extends the DPA reference calculator

The Digital Pathology Association publishes a popular ROI reference calculator. Turoi covers every DPA input and adds the following rigor:

  • Monte Carlo simulation on every pillar — you get a 5th–95th percentile confidence interval, not a point estimate.
  • NPV + IRR + discounting — DPA reports nominal multi-year sums; Turoi discounts future cash flows at your organization's actual cost of capital.
  • Regional defaults for India, US, and UK across 12+ institutional sub-profiles, including government medical colleges, corporate hospitals, and reference labs.
  • Citation ledger — every default has a source URL, confidence level (high/medium/low), and reasoning. Filter, export to CSV/BibTeX, or edit inline.
  • Tornado sensitivity analysis — ±20% perturbation on every input, ranked by NPV impact.
  • Profile-aware visibility — wizard sections appear only when relevant to your profile type and region.
  • Scanner capacity feasibility check — warns if projected slide volume exceeds realistic scanner throughput (DPA silently accepts infeasible projections).
  • Telepathology revenue pillar — models client ramp, volume compounding, and fee escalation for labs monetizing remote reporting (absent from DPA).
  • AI-analyst advisor — interactive what-if chat that re-runs the projection live.

Every field listed in the DPA calculator is tracked in Turoi. Shared inputs use the same definitions; Turoi adds on-prem/cloud storage split, workstation breakdown, scanner capacity, legal tripartite breakdown, and seven DPA pillars (ROSE, QC, IT architecture, education, collaboration, commercialization, US CPT reimbursement).
